Salem Chapel

We are all creating a Living Heritage as we live our daily lives.

The East Budleigh Heritage Centre in Salem Chapel aims to seek out social and educational connections between the past and the present which we can enjoy by ourselves and with family and friends.

East Budleigh Heritage Centre Ltd is operating a community centre in Salem Chapel under license from the Historic Chapels Trust for six moths to end December 2023 and acts as the local key holder. The Historic Chapels Trust is obliged to find a new owner for Salem Chapel by March 2025 and is in the process of completing a detailed survey report for the sale prospectus. A number of potential new owners are being assessed and contacted ahead of the formal invitation in autumn 2023.

Following an approach from the East Budleigh History Society to enquire into the use of Salem Chapel in February, an excellent relationship with HCT has been established. On learning of the upcoming sale, the Society conducted a village survey in March resulting in 106 responses, via the East Budleigh Parish Magazine and via Survey Monkey online on the Village WhatsApp Groups (222 participants). The community meeting in April concluded that the village was keen to have use of Salem Chapel but did not have the funds to take ownership as a community asset. Both the Community Shop and the Sir Walter Raleigh pub are successful community-owned businesses.

Our heritage and culture has evolved through societies over the years, with arts, craft, music and literature defining ever-changing values. The villages, towns and cities where we live are where we form communities and a shared identity; these constantly evolve. Our places of work - farms, mills, workshops, factories, offices and homes - and our transport infrastructure, mark the passage of time.

We are all part of the Living Heritage in our daily lives as we seek out social and educational connections between the past and the present.
